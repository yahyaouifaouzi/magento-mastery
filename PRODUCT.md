# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

E-commerce store owners, technical founders, and marketing managers who need Magento 2 development expertise. They've experienced unreliable freelancers or agencies that over-promised. They're looking for a specialist who understands Magento deeply — not a generalist. They value clear communication, fixed pricing, and code that doesn't break on upgrade.

## Product Purpose

A portfolio and marketing site for a Magento 2 freelance specialist. It exists to convert technical expertise into client trust. Success is a visitor arriving, reading a blog post or service page, and contacting the owner for a project quote.

## Brand Personality

Expert, Precise, Confident. The voice assumes the reader already knows they need a Magento specialist; the site doesn't sell "why Magento" but "why this developer." Technical depth is worn lightly — the blog posts prove competence through substance, not jargon.

## Anti-references

- Over-animated, gimmicky "wow" websites where motion replaces substance
- Generic freelance marketplace profiles (Upwork, Fiverr) — cookie-cutter, no differentiation
- Template-looking agency sites with stock photos and vague promises
- "AI-designed" visual clichés: gradient text, glassmorphism as default, numbered section eyebrows, hero-metric templates

## Design Principles

1. **Expert confidence, not showmanship** — The site proves expertise through depth of content and clean execution, not visual tricks. Motion is intentional and restrained, never decorative.

2. **Code that survives upgrades** — The design philosophy mirrors the service promise: no shortcuts, no hacks, no core rewrites. Every component should feel as solid as well-architected Magento code.

3. **Clear scope, fixed price** — Every page has a clear job: inform, build trust, or prompt contact. No ambiguity, no clutter. The user should never wonder what to do next.

4. **Dark first, not dark default** — Dark mode is the primary theme because the audience (technical, often working in dark IDEs/terminals at night) naturally prefers it. Light mode is a considered alternative, not an afterthought.

5. **Show, don't tell** — The blog and portfolio do the selling. Service pages describe what's delivered, not how skilled the developer is. Let the work speak.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

- WCAG AA contrast minimum across both themes
- Skip-to-content link, semantic HTML, keyboard-navigable nav
- Reduced-motion alternative for all animations
- Form validation with clear error messages
